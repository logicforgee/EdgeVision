// HOW IT WORKS

From Raw Pixels to
Intent in 47ms

EdgeVision runs a multi-stage perception-to-prediction pipeline entirely on the vehicle's onboard GPU — no cloud round-trips, no latency penalty.

01

Multi-Camera Sensor Fusion

RGB frames from up to 8 surround cameras are synchronized and fused with LiDAR point clouds for depth-aware scene understanding at 30 FPS.

02

Real-Time Pedestrian Detection & Tracking

A lightweight YOLOv8-nano model detects and assigns persistent track IDs to every pedestrian, maintaining identity across occlusions.

03

3D Skeleton Keypoint Extraction

HRNet-W32 lifts 17 COCO keypoints into 3D using disparity maps — capturing head orientation, gaze direction, and full body pose.

04

Temporal GRU Intention Classifier

A Bi-directional GRU processes a 1-second pose history (30 frames) and outputs crossing probability every 47ms — enabling proactive braking.

05

Decision Signal to AV Planner

Intent labels and confidence scores are published to the ROS2 planning stack in real time, triggering yield, slow, or emergency response modes.

// CAPABILITIES

Built for the Edge,
Not the Cloud

⚡

Sub-50ms Inference

Entire pipeline runs in under 47ms on NVIDIA Jetson Orin NX — fast enough to trigger braking at city speeds (50 km/h = 0.65m reaction gain).

🦴

3D Skeleton Pose Tracking

Extracts 17 COCO keypoints per pedestrian in full 3D using stereo depth, capturing head turn, gaze vector, and body lean as intent cues.

⏳

Temporal Sequence Modeling

Bi-directional GRU processes 30-frame (1-second) pose histories, capturing the micro-motions that precede a crossing decision by 1–2 seconds.

🌐

Multi-Pedestrian Parallel Tracking

Independently tracks and predicts intent for up to 16 simultaneous pedestrians per frame using batched inference on the embedded GPU.

🚨

Risk-Prioritized Output

Outputs a calibrated crossing probability per pedestrian, letting the AV planner prioritize the highest-risk agent and tune safety thresholds.

🌙

Night & Adverse Weather Robust

Trained on diverse lighting (night, rain, snow, glare) and validated on OCC-Ped and nuScenes-Pedestrian for real-world robustness.

📦

ONNX & TensorRT Export

Models export to ONNX and TensorRT INT8 for deployment on any automotive-grade SoC — Orin, Xavier, Snapdragon Ride, or custom.

🔗

ROS2 Native Integration

Intent predictions publish directly to ROS2 topics as custom message types, integrating cleanly with Autoware, Apollo, and custom AV stacks.

📊

Uncertainty Quantification

Outputs Bayesian confidence intervals alongside point predictions — letting the planner treat uncertain predictions conservatively.

// RESEARCH & DATASETS

Validated on
Real-World Data

EdgeVision is trained and evaluated on the most challenging public pedestrian intention benchmarks.

DATASET

JAAD — Joint Attention in Autonomous Driving

346 urban driving clips, 2,786 pedestrian tracks with behavioural annotations for crossing intention, attention, and action.

DATASET

PIE — Pedestrian Intention Estimation

6 hours of continuous urban driving, 1,842 pedestrian tracks, ego-velocity data, and traffic signal context for multi-modal prediction.

DATASET

nuScenes-Pedestrian

Multi-city LiDAR+camera dataset with 1,000 driving scenes and dense 3D bounding box annotations for sensor fusion training.

BASELINE MODEL

PedGraph+ (CVPR 2023)

Graph-based pedestrian trajectory prediction using spatiotemporal skeleton graphs. Serves as a key comparison baseline.

BASELINE MODEL

PCPA — Pedestrian Context-Aware Prediction

Multi-context fusion model (pose, scene, ego-motion) from IROS 2021. EdgeVision outperforms it while running 4× faster on edge hardware.

BASELINE MODEL

BiPed (IEEE TITS 2022)

Bidirectional LSTM for crossing prediction. EdgeVision uses a similar temporal backbone but extends it with 3D pose and scene context.
